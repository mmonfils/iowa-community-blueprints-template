# The Determinants Framework: Community Fact-Finding Guide

This framework helps independent data researchers map local friction points using free, public open records. It replaces sterile academic jargon with three clear, visible levels of neighborhood impact.

---

## Level 1: The Macro Level (Broad Decisions)
Look at the wide institutional choices, regional legislation, or city ordinances dictating how resources move across the county. 

* **What to track:** Record explicit council votes, zoning board maps, and upcoming committee deadlines. 
* **The Goal:** Pinpoint exactly which administrative body has the formal power to edit or amend the active rule.

## Level 2: The Transmission Level (Local Channels)
Track how those broad, top-down choices are passed down into real-world neighborhood environments. 

* **What to track:** Document the specific municipal agencies, private contractors, or enforcement loops handling daily execution.
* **The Goal:** Locate the operational bottleneck or administrative friction point where service gaps or billing issues occur.

## Level 3: The Micro Level (Immediate Blocks)
Document the concrete, everyday impact felt by residents along a specific street corridor or housing cluster.

* **What to track:** Record localized timing delays, personal medication disruptions, or immediate housing instability numbers.
* **The Goal:** Anchor the research to human terms, proving exactly how macro-level capital choices alter daily neighborhood safety.
